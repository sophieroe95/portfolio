About

Education:

I grew up in Bristol, I studied sciences at A-Level and was always interested in technology. I chose to study medicine at University as I enjoyed applying science to real-life problems and wanted to help other people. Although I enjoyed the constant learning and meeting new people, I wanted a role with more in-depth problem solving and creativity. I decided I did not want to pursue becoming a doctor so left the course, graduating with a 2.1 in Medical Studies. Important skills I learnt while in education:

  • Resilience
  • Teamwork
  • Problem Solving

Work Experience:

I worked as a District Nurse administrator and found I was interested in the IT aspect of this role, such as how patient data was stored. I then worked as a data administrator at The University of West of England (UWE) where I enjoyed analysing detailed information. Lastly, I worked as a Data Administrator at a Student Accomadation Company where I aided in the testing of a new website. I got to work with software developers here and was intrigued by the code. After doing some research and online coding courses I decided to enter the _nology course. Important skills I learnt whilst working:
